Cold Fusion Couloir

Believe it or not, I skied this stupid thing. Owen said it had more bark than bite, but that's because he's a great skier. I think it had both bark and plenty of bite.

I had the place all to myself the day I did it. It was a day late in the season when some people were done skiing, but the couloir was plenty filled in because we had had a mega super record-breaking snow year.

I recall how pathetic my ascent was, not to mention my descent, which I'll get to in a moment. Going up the thing was a long booter, and it nearly killed me because of how horribly out of shape I am. It was also a bit nerve racking, because it is quite steep. Ice axe and crampons were a good choice the day. I think I gave myself an over-use injury by the time I finally draggy my sorry butt to the top.

As for the descent, all I can really say is that I did ski it. I skied it very badly, but I did ski it, and I didn't side-slip it or take my skis off at any time. I stopped about a zillion times to catch my breath because I was hyper-ventillating (more from being out of breath than scared...I think.)

The steepness really wasn't a problem for me; it was the consequences. If you blew a turn, and didn't self arrest, you'd accelerate down the slope, faster and faster, until you hit rocks or trees and died.

In any case, I'm glad I did it, and it was neat. I'm embarrassed to say that once I finally got to the bottom, I think I charley-horsed really badly. I guess I should be glad that happened after I had gotten down.

I think I lost all pictures of this, but if I find one, I'll put it up.
